    Provincial Capitol Lagoon operated by the Negros Occidental Provincial Government is a pit-stop of city tours in Bacolod City. This Capitol Park and lagoon is located in a three-hectare land area in Lacson Street, Bacolod City. With its vast open area, locals and tourists love visiting the capitol park to jog, exercise and simply stroll. Families also hold picnics at the park especially during weekends and holidays.

    Bacolod Plaza is one of the famous landmarks of Bacolod City as it commemorates the day when the Spanish surrendered Negros Island to the Negros Revolutionaries on November 6, 1898, giving it the official name of Plaza del 6 de Noviembre. The plaza is home to four circular fountains scattered around the park, along with a gazebo at the center where performances are held during festivals or events.

    The New Government Center or the NGC is now one of the new landmarks of Bacolod City. It stands on a 5 hectare lot donated by Fernando F. Gonzaga, Inc. and Angela Estate, Inc. Inaugurated in July 2010, it replaced the old City Hall at Araneta-Luriaga Sreets. It costed the City and its people almost 400 million to build this new seat of power. Since the start of operation of the NGC, it has attracted business and trade in the surrounding area.

    SM City Bacolod is a supermall located along Rizal Street, Reclamation Area, Bacolod, Negros Occidental, Philippines. SM City Bacolod is the 29th supermall of SM Prime Holdings, the 1st SM supermall in Negros Island Region and the 3rd in the Visayas. It opened on March 1, 2007 and currently has a total land area of 161,096.60 square metres (1,734,029.4 sq ft) and a total floor area of 133,894 square metres (1,441,220 sq ft). SM City Bacolod is currently undergoing renovation and expansion that will be completed in phases.
