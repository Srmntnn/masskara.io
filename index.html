    If you are looking for a festival that is full of color, music, and dance, then the MassKara Festival is definitely worth checking out. It is a truly unique experience that you will never forget.

    About Masskara

    The MassKara Festival is an annual festival with highlights held every 4th Sunday of October in Bacolod, Philippines. The festival is a celebration of the city's resilience and optimism, and is known for its colorful masks and vibrant street dancing.

    The festival was first held in 1980, during a time when the Philippines was facing economic and political challenges. The people of Bacolod decided to create a festival that would showcase the city's culture and spirit, and bring joy to its residents.

    The MassKara Festival features a variety of events, including the MassKara Queen beauty pageant, carnivals, drum and bugle corps competitions, food festivals, sports events, musical concerts, and agricultural trade fairs. The highlight of the festival is the MassKara Street Dancing Competition, where groups of dancers wearing colorful masks and costumes perform choreographed routines to Latin music.

    Here are some of the things you can expect when you attend the MassKara Festival:

    Colorful Mask

    The MassKara Festival is known for its colorful masks, which are worn by everyone from the dancers to the spectators. The masks are often decorated with beads, sequins, and feathers, and represent the joy and optimism of the people of Bacolod.

    Vibrant Street Dancing

    The MassKara Street Dancing Competition is the highlight of the festival, and features groups of dancers performing choreographed routines to Latin music. The dancers wear colorful costumes and masks, and the competition is a feast for the eyes.

    Festive Atmosphere

    The MassKara Festival is a celebration of life, and the atmosphere is electric. The streets are filled with people dancing, singing, and enjoying themselves. There is also a variety of food and drinks available, and the festival is a great place to experience the Filipino culture.
